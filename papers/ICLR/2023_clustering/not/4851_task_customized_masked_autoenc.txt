  "summary": "This paper addresses the problem of negative transfer in self-supervised pretraining models like Masked Autoencoders (MAE) when applied to semantically different downstream tasks. It proposes Mixture of Cluster-Conditional Experts (MoCE), which first clusters the data based on semantic similarity and then trains different experts on these clusters. A customized routing mechanism ensures that each downstream task uses the most semantically relevant expert. Experiments show MoCE outperforms MAE by 2.45% on transfer tasks and achieves state-of-the-art results in object detection and segmentation benchmarks. It also offers training and deployment efficiencies by activating only relevant experts.",
